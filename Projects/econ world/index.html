About Soulland

Soulland is a island nation built on hybrid capitalism and communal values. Our society is home to three unique populations: the Normies who excel in every-day life, the Pacifists who nurture our agricultural heritage, and the Vivacious, our naturally gifted leaders born with complete knowledge.

We are a country similar to Canada in many ways: utilizing mixed-economy principles, celebrating multiculturalism, maintaining strong social values, and relying on our abundant natural resources. However, what truly makes Soulland unique is our diverse populations, our innovative economy, and our commitment to resource sharing across all social classes.

Our flag represents our three cultures: the human figure symbolizes the Normies, the pawprint honors the Pacifists' love for animals and nature, and the two hearts represent the enlarged souls of the Vivacious who lead our nation.

Our country is at peace with it's two neighboring countries. Trading over any prisoners to Luvocity and paying Artoria to keep Luvocity in check. Additonally, Artoria supplies boats and fishing gear to Soulland.

Three Unique Populations

Mixed Economy

Soulland combines capitalist market mechanisms with communist social values, creating a balanced system where citizens work for income but freely share resources with their community.

Rich Natural Resources

From copper and iron mines to wheat fields and rare fish, Soulland's natural wealth expands our economy and supports international trade relationships.

Global Export Leader

We export mining products, agricultural goods, rare fish, and most notably, our Vivacious talent providing military and governmental services worth $1.2 billion annually.

Sustainable Future

Through reforestation campaigns from THE MR. BEAST, resource management policies, and immigration plans, Soulland works toward long-term environmental and economic sustainability.

Key Economic Facts

  • GDP Per Capita: $24,000 USD - Upper-middle income nation
  • Major Exports: Vivacious services ($1.2B), copper, rare fish, silver, wheat
  • Trade Surplus: Approximately $1.4 billion annually
  • Primary Industries: Mining (20% GDP), Agriculture (12% GDP), Government Services (35% GDP)
  • Employment: Nearly full employment across all three populations
  • Technology: Heavy investment in technology for productivity
  • Immigration: Currently limited; campaigns underway to attract skilled workers
  • Environmental Focus: Forest preservation and reforestation are critical priorities
